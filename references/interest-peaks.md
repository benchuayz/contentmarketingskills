# Interest Peaks

30+ mid-content re-engagement triggers that prevent viewers from scrolling away. Use these at natural transition points to recapture attention.

**When to use:** Insert 1-2 interest peaks per short-form script (at the ~30% and ~60% marks). For long-form content, use every 2-3 minutes. Never force them — they should feel like a natural part of the conversation.

---

## 1. Authority Endorsement

Borrow credibility from known names or impressive results to re-establish trust mid-content.

**1.1** "This is a technique I learned from [respected figure] that literally [desirable outcome]"
- "This is a technique I learned from Alex Hormozi that literally doubled my close rate"
- "This is a technique I learned from my first mentor that literally changed how I think about money"

**1.2** "This is the same way [company or person] [achieved impressive result]"
- "This is the same way Nike generated $100M in their Black Friday campaign"
- "This is the same way the top 1% of creators build their audience"

**1.3** "And if you don't believe me, [authority figure] has been using this [strategy] for [timeframe]"
- "And if you don't believe me, the top sales teams in the world have been using this framework for decades"
- "And if you don't believe me, every $100M+ brand uses this exact approach"

**1.4** "And this is coming from someone who [achieved impressive result]"
- "And this is coming from someone who went from broke to the top 1% by 25"
- "And this is coming from someone who's closed over $2M in sales this year"

**1.5** "And this is coming from someone who [achieved result] despite [having disadvantage]"
- "And I grew my portfolio to $300K despite growing up broke and financially illiterate"
- "And I built a 6-figure business despite having zero marketing experience"

---

## 2. Exclusion / Callout

Create an "in-group vs out-group" dynamic. Viewers want to be in the smart group.

**2.1** "But so many [target audience] fall into this trap"
- "But so many new creators fall into this trap"
- "But so many guys fall into this trap"
- "But so many first-time founders fall into this trap"

**2.2** "This is where most people fuck up"
- Direct, no niche variation needed. Works universally.

**2.3** "First, don't [common mistake]. Instead, [solution]"
- "First, don't try to justify your price on a sales call. Instead, shut up and let the silence do the work"
- "First, don't start your video with context. Instead, open with the result"

**2.4** "And no, I'm not going to tell you to [obvious advice] because you already know you need to do that"
- "And no, I'm not going to tell you to make more content or send more DMs because you already know you need to do that"
- "And no, I'm not going to tell you to write better headlines or track more data because you already know you need to do that"

**2.5** "[X out of 10] people do this. Don't be one of them"
- "8 out of 10 guys do this. Don't be one of them"
- "9 out of 10 sales reps do this. Don't be one of them"

---

## 3. Risk Reversal

Reduce perceived effort/cost/risk to keep them watching.

**3.1** "And the crazy thing is this will literally take you under a minute to set up while costing you less than the price of lunch"
- Works for any low-barrier tactic. Adjust the time and cost to fit.

**3.2** "And you don't need [beneficial prerequisite 1] or [beneficial prerequisite 2] to do it"
- "And you don't need discipline or motivation to do it"
- "And you don't need sales experience or great communication skills to do it"
- "And you don't need money or connections to do it"

**3.3** "And don't worry, this won't [cause negative outcome]. It might even [cause the opposite]"
- "And don't worry, this won't get you fired. It might even get you promoted"
- "And don't worry, this won't ruin your metabolism. It might even speed it up"

**3.4** "And I'll make it so simple that a kid could do it"
- Universal. Works for any tutorial or how-to content.

**3.5** "And I promise you, not only does it work, but it costs you nothing"
- Best for free tools, strategies, or mindset shifts.

---

## 4. Hype / Momentum

Build excitement and energy. These accelerate the pacing right when attention might drop.

**4.1** "And it gets even better..."
- Simple, powerful. Use it before revealing the second or third benefit.

**4.2** "But there are too many people [achieving result] off [method] for me not to make this video"
- "But there are too many people getting rich off this AI tool for me not to make this video"
- "But there are too many founders building audiences with this strategy for me not to share it"

**4.3** "This is a concept I spent [X] years learning and I still use it every day"
- "This is a concept I spent 10 years learning when I was building my first business and I still use it every day"

**4.4** "I was gonna gatekeep this, but fuck it. I'll give it to you for free"
- Creates instant perceived value through manufactured scarcity.

**4.5** "This works for anyone regardless of [qualification they're worried about]"
- "This works for anyone regardless of whether you went to college or not"
- "This works for anyone regardless of your follower count"

**4.6** "If you've ever wondered how [successful type] is doing it, we're gonna steal it... together"
- "If you've ever wondered how luxury brands are doing it, we're gonna steal it... together"
- "If you've ever wondered how top creators monetize, we're gonna steal it... together"

---

## 5. Controversy / Challenge

Stir emotion. Make them FEEL something — agreement, disagreement, or surprise.

**5.1** "And yet, 99% of the people watching won't make it to the end of this video"
- Reverse psychology. Challenges the viewer's ego.

**5.2** "And before you get upset, hear me out"
- Signals something provocative is coming. Builds anticipation.

**5.3** "And I know that what I'm about to say might make everyone upset"
- Same energy. Creates tension before the reveal.

**5.4** "Don't get me wrong, [accepted method] is good. But [alternative] is just so much better"
- "Don't get me wrong, running ads for leads is good. But building a brand that sells for you is just so much better"
- "Don't get me wrong, going to the gym 6 days a week is good. But training smart 4 days a week is just so much better"

**5.5** "Now this won't work for you if you're [weak trait], but if you're [strong trait] and you're serious about [goal], then pay attention"
- "Now this won't work for you if you're looking for shortcuts, but if you're serious about building real wealth, then pay attention"
- Creates self-selection. Viewer doesn't want to be in the "weak" group.

---

## 6. Personal Proof / Story

Share specific results or moments that make the advice concrete and trustworthy.

**6.1** "I've done this for [time period] and it's gotten me to places I used to dream about"
- "I've done this for the last 2 years and it's gotten me to places I used to dream about"

**6.2** "And just so you know it's not bullshit, here's [my results] in the past [X] days"
- "And just so you know it's not bullshit, here's how much money I've made in the past 7 days"
- "And just so you know it's not bullshit, here's how many clients I've booked in the past 3 days"

**6.3** "These [insights] have allowed me to [impressive achievement]"
- "These lessons have allowed me to create over $1M in sales in the last 3 months"
- "These steps have allowed me to grow from 0 to 50K followers in 6 months"

**6.4** "I've just spent [X time] analyzing [X things] to give you this information"
- "I've just spent 5 hours analyzing 20,000 videos to give you this information"
- "I've just spent 2 weeks analyzing 150 ad campaigns to give you this information"

**6.5** "And this is huge if you want to [desirable outcome] fast"
- Simple momentum builder. Bridges to the next section.

---

## Placement Guide

### Short-Form Content (60-90 seconds)
- **Peak 1:** After the hook, around 15-20 seconds in (re-establish credibility or open a second loop)
- **Peak 2:** Around 40-50 seconds (before the big reveal or key insight)

### Long-Form Content (YouTube)
- **Every 2-3 minutes:** Rotate between categories
- **Before major transitions:** Use a peak to bridge sections
- **After delivering value:** Use authority or proof to reinforce what you just taught

### General Rules
- Never use the same category twice in a row
- Don't force them — if the content flows naturally, you might only need 1
- Mix energy levels: a calm authority endorsement followed by a high-energy hype trigger
- Always make them FEEL something specific: curiosity, urgency, FOMO, or excitement
