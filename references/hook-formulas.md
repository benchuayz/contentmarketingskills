# Hook Formulas

50+ proven scroll-stopping hooks organized by psychological trigger. Each formula includes the template, 3 niche examples, and why it works.

**How to use:** Pick a category that matches your content goal. Choose a formula. Fill in the blanks with your niche, numbers, and specifics. The more specific you are, the better the hook performs.

---

## 1. Contrarian Hooks

Challenge what everyone believes. These work because they create cognitive dissonance — the viewer HAS to keep watching to resolve the tension.

### 1.1 The Myth Buster
**Formula:** "One of the biggest scams in [niche] is [popular thing]"

- "One of the biggest scams in real estate is a will"
- "One of the biggest scams in fitness is protein powder"
- "One of the biggest scams in investing is brokerage fees"

**Why it works:** Cialdini's authority principle inverted — challenges the authority of conventional wisdom. Creates an open loop the viewer needs to close.

### 1.2 The Reverse Expert
**Formula:** "You need to stop [doing action] in [niche] if you want [desired result]"

- "You need to stop door-knocking as a real estate agent if you want to sell houses in 2025"
- "You need to stop counting calories if you want to lose weight permanently"
- "You need to stop cold emailing if you want clients that actually pay premium rates"

**Why it works:** Pattern interrupt — tells people to STOP something they believe is helping them.

### 1.3 The "It's Dead" Declaration
**Formula:** "[Common strategy] is dead. You need to try [alternative] instead"

- "Dropshipping is dead. You need to try this untouched AI business model instead"
- "Cold outreach is dead. You need to build a content engine instead"
- "LinkedIn carousels are dead. You need to try text-only posts instead"

**Why it works:** Loss aversion — viewer fears they're wasting time on a dead strategy.

### 1.4 The Uncomfortable Truth
**Formula:** "You don't have [common excuse]. You just [real reason]"

- "You don't have a fast metabolism. You just don't eat enough"
- "You don't have bad hooks. You just aren't giving enough value"
- "You don't have the wrong investment strategy. You just aren't patient"

**Why it works:** Removes the viewer's comfortable excuse and forces self-reflection.

### 1.5 The Controversial Trigger
**Formula:** "If [popular thing] weren't a normal thing, it would be a banned [substance/practice]"

- "If sleep weren't a normal thing, it would be a banned performance drug"
- "If compound interest weren't built into banking, it would be considered a cheat code"
- "If video content weren't free to make, it would be the most expensive ad channel that exists"

**Why it works:** Reframes something ordinary as extraordinary. Creates a "holy shit" moment.

### 1.6 The Paradigm Shift
**Formula:** "[Topic] is not about [what everyone thinks] vs. [what everyone thinks]"

- "Sales is not about you vs. the customer"
- "Content is not about quality vs. quantity"
- "Fitness is not about motivation vs. discipline"

**Why it works:** Breaks a false dichotomy, making the viewer curious about the real answer.

### 1.7 The Industry Callout
**Formula:** "This is the cheat code of cheat codes. [Demographic] is going to be mad when I show you this"

- "This is the cheat code of cheat codes. Fitness coaches are going to be mad when I show you this"
- "This is the cheat code of cheat codes. Marketing agencies are going to be mad when I show you this"
- "This is the cheat code of cheat codes. Real estate agents are going to be mad when I show you this"

**Why it works:** Creates in-group/out-group tension. The viewer feels like they're getting insider access.

### 1.8 The Permission Slip
**Formula:** "Not taking advantage of [overlooked thing] is doing a huge disservice to [something they care about]"

- "Not taking advantage of the US credit system is doing a huge disservice to your business"
- "Not taking advantage of free AI tools is doing a huge disservice to your content output"
- "Not taking advantage of YouTube SEO is doing a huge disservice to your future self"

**Why it works:** Turns inaction into active self-harm. Creates urgency through loss framing.

## 2. Credibility Hooks

Lead with proof. These work because authority is the fastest shortcut to trust — when someone has results, you listen.

### 2.1 The Anti-Humble Brag
**Formula:** "Not to flex but I'm pretty fucking good at [skill]. If I had to [start over], here's what I'd do"

- "Not to flex but I'm pretty good at social media marketing. If I had to start my page over from scratch, here's what I'd do"
- "Not to flex but I'm pretty good at day trading. If I had to start again with just $100, here's what I'd do"
- "Not to flex but I'm pretty good at making money. If I had to start a business with less than $500, here's what I'd do"

**Why it works:** Combines authority with a relatable scenario (starting over). The "not to flex" acknowledges the brag, making it feel honest.

### 2.2 The Time Investment
**Formula:** "I've spent [X hours/years] [doing research]. Don't waste your time — here's the [X] that actually [work]"

- "I've spent 100+ hours testing different AI prompts. Don't waste your time — here's the 3 that actually saved me money"
- "I've spent 237 hours on cold calls testing different openers. Don't waste your time — here's the 3 that actually lead to sales"
- "I've spent over 50 hours using different editing apps. Don't waste your time — here's the 3 that will actually improve your videos"

**Why it works:** You've done the work so they don't have to. Social proof + reciprocity combined.

### 2.3 The Decade Lesson
**Formula:** "It took me [X] years to master [skill], but I'm going to teach you the [X] most powerful lessons in [X] seconds"

- "It took me 5 years to master communication, but I'll teach you the 5 most powerful lessons in 60 seconds"
- "It took me 2 years to master productivity, but I'll teach you the 3 most powerful lessons in 45 seconds"
- "It took me 3 years to master copywriting, but I'll teach you the 5 most powerful lessons in 53 seconds"

**Why it works:** Compresses years of experience into seconds. Massive perceived value.

### 2.4 The Specific Result
**Formula:** "I [get measurable result] every [time period]. Here's how"

- "I make an Etsy sale every 45 minutes. Here's how"
- "I book 3 sales calls every day. Here's how"
- "I gain 500 followers every week. Here's how"

**Why it works:** Specificity = credibility. Vague claims get ignored. Specific numbers stop the scroll.

### 2.5 The Proof Stack
**Formula:** "After [long time] of [doing action], here's what I wish someone would've told me from day one"

- "After over a decade of lifting, here's what I wish someone would've told me from day one"
- "After 8 years in sales, here's what I wish someone would've told me from day one"
- "After 12 months running Facebook ads, here's what I wish someone would've told me from day one"

**Why it works:** Positions you as battle-tested. The "from day one" implies you'll save them years.

### 2.6 The Curated List
**Formula:** "I've [consumed] over [X] [sources] on [topic]. 50% were useless, 25% were just wrong, but [X] really changed how I think about [niche] forever"

- "I've listened to over 1000 podcasts on making money. 50% were useless, 25% were just wrong, but 11 really changed how I think about finance forever"
- "I've read over 50 books on persuasion. 50% were useless, 25% were just wrong, but 7 really changed how I think about sales forever"
- "I've watched over 1000 videos on social media growth. 50% were useless, 25% were just wrong, but 5 really changed how I think about content creation forever"

**Why it works:** You filtered the noise. Viewer gets the curated best of the best.

### 2.7 The Live Proof
**Formula:** "I only [do action] [limited amount] and I still [have impressive result]. Here's why you don't need more time — you need more [insight]"

- "I only go to the gym 4x per week and I still look like this. You don't need more time — you need more structure"
- "I only work 4 hours a day and I still make $15k/month. You don't need more time — you need more leverage"
- "I only post 3x a week and I still signed 5 new clients. You don't need more time — you need more direction"

**Why it works:** Challenges the "hustle harder" narrative. Positions insight over effort.

## 3. Curiosity Hooks

Open a loop that can only be closed by watching. These exploit the Zeigarnik effect — unfinished business is psychologically unbearable.

### 3.1 The Forbidden Knowledge
**Formula:** "I probably shouldn't be sharing this because people can abuse it, but here's how you can..."

- "I probably shouldn't be sharing this because agencies can abuse it, but here's how you can get premium content for free"
- "I probably shouldn't be sharing this because it gives you an unfair advantage, but here's how you can win every negotiation"
- "I probably shouldn't be sharing this because my competitors will hate me, but here's how you can steal their strategy"

**Why it works:** Scarcity + exclusivity. "Forbidden" information feels more valuable.

### 3.2 The Unknown Concept
**Formula:** "Remember [unknown, simple-sounding concept] for your next [action]"

- "Remember light-light-dark for your next outfit"
- "Remember 3-1-1 for your next workout"
- "Remember the 30% rule for your next sales call"

**Why it works:** Creates an instant knowledge gap. What IS the 30% rule? Must watch to find out.

### 3.3 The Gatekeeper Callout
**Formula:** "Why are so many people gatekeeping the real way to [reach goal]?"

- "Why are so many people gatekeeping the real way to go viral?"
- "Why are so many people gatekeeping the real way to negotiate a salary raise?"
- "Why are so many people gatekeeping the real way to get brand deals?"

**Why it works:** Creates conspiracy energy. Viewer feels excluded from secret knowledge.

### 3.4 The Surprising Connection
**Formula:** "What if I told you this [simple thing] could [get you a measurable result]"

- "What if I told you this berry could take 12 minutes off your marathon time"
- "What if I told you this website could get you 350% more sales"
- "What if I told you this one word could add 8% to the value of your home"

**Why it works:** The gap between the simple cause and the impressive effect is irresistible.

### 3.5 The Incomplete Reveal
**Formula:** "The reason you can't [achieve goal] isn't because of [excuse 1] or [excuse 2]. It's because you're missing..."

- "The reason you're stuck creating content under 10K followers isn't because of your video quality or your editing. It's because you're missing..."
- "The reason you can't close deals under $5k/month isn't because your niche is saturated or your messaging. It's because you're missing..."
- "The reason you're stuck yo-yo dieting isn't because of your hormones or your metabolism. It's because you're missing..."

**Why it works:** Eliminates their current excuses then opens a massive curiosity loop.

## 4. Pain/Negative Hooks

Agitate a problem they already feel. These work because pain is 2x more motivating than pleasure (prospect theory).

### 4.1 The Fatal Mistake
**Formula:** "Here is a deadly mistake beginners make when it comes to [niche action]"

- "Here is a deadly mistake beginners make when it comes to squatting"
- "Here is a deadly mistake beginners make when it comes to pricing their services"
- "Here is a deadly mistake beginners make when it comes to writing LinkedIn posts"

**Why it works:** Fear of making the mistake themselves. Nobody wants to be the beginner who gets it wrong.

### 4.2 The Direct Callout
**Formula:** "Don't tell me [common excuse/complaint]"

- "Don't tell me eating healthy is too expensive"
- "Don't tell me your leads aren't good"
- "Don't tell me the algorithm hates you"

**Why it works:** Aggressive pattern interrupt. Calls out their inner narrative directly.

### 4.3 The Red Flag Warning
**Formula:** "These are the top 5 red flags to look out for in a [niche relationship]"

- "These are 5 red flags to look out for in a personal trainer"
- "These are 5 red flags to look out for in a marketing agency"
- "These are 5 red flags to look out for in a business partner"

**Why it works:** Protective instinct kicks in. Everyone wants to avoid being scammed.

### 4.4 The Worst Case List
**Formula:** "5 worst [things] to [do] right now and the one you should [do] instead"

- "5 worst online businesses to start right now and the one you should start instead"
- "5 worst investments to make right now and the one you should make instead"
- "5 worst types of posts to create right now and the one you should create instead"

**Why it works:** Double hook — fear of doing the wrong thing + curiosity about the right thing.

### 4.5 The Age Pressure
**Formula:** "If you're [age range], these are the [X] things you need to do to make sure you don't end up [undesirable outcome] by the time you're [age]"

- "If you're in your 20s, these are the 5 things you need to do to make sure you don't end up broke by 35"
- "If you're in your 30s, these are the 5 things you need to do to make sure you don't end up with lifelong back pain by 40"
- "If you're in your 20s, these are the 3 things you need to do so you don't look back at this decade with regret"

**Why it works:** Time urgency + identity targeting. Age-specific hooks feel personal.

### 4.6 The Painful Truth
**Formula:** "If you doubted for a second that [bold claim], watch this..."

- "If you doubted for a second that dropshipping is still the fastest way to make $10k/month, watch this"
- "If you doubted for a second that AI is coming for your job, watch this"
- "If you doubted for a second that content is the best lead generation strategy, watch this"

**Why it works:** Challenges the viewer's skepticism directly. "I dare you to watch and not be convinced."

## 5. Story Hooks

Stories bypass the brain's sales filter. These work because humans are wired for narrative — we can't help but follow a story to its conclusion.

### 5.1 The Relatable Struggle
**Formula:** "So, just like you, I used to struggle with [common problem] until [turning point]"

- "So, just like you, I struggled with waking up early until I found this sunlight alarm clock"
- "So, just like you, I struggled with creating consistent content until I built this system"
- "So, just like you, I struggled with closing sales calls until I changed one thing about my opening"

**Why it works:** Immediate identification ("just like you") + clear transformation arc.

### 5.2 The Late Realization
**Formula:** "It took me [X] years to realize [insight]"

- "It took me 8 years to realize how to actually make $10k/month"
- "It took me 5 years to realize most business advice is designed to keep you broke"
- "It took me 3 years to realize the secret to going viral has nothing to do with the algorithm"

**Why it works:** Implies deep, hard-won wisdom. The "years" adds weight.

### 5.3 The Confession
**Formula:** "I just found out that I've been doing [action] wrong my whole life"

- "I just found out that I've been doing sales wrong my whole life"
- "I just found out that I've been stretching wrong my whole life"
- "I just found out that I've been writing hooks wrong my whole life"

**Why it works:** Vulnerability + curiosity. If an expert can be wrong, what about the viewer?

### 5.4 The Social Proof Story
**Formula:** "My [person close to you] was confused when I started doing this, but now [they] aren't because it's [paying for something]"

- "My wife was confused when I started doing this but now she isn't because it's paying off our mortgage"
- "My parents were confused when I started doing this but now they aren't because it's paying for their retirement"
- "My friends were confused when I started doing this but now they aren't because it's paying for us to fly first class"

**Why it works:** Third-party validation. The skeptic became a believer — so can you.

### 5.5 The Hard-Won Lesson
**Formula:** "I've made this video 10 times and I'll make it 100 more if it gets across to the whole internet"

- Works best as a standalone — no niche variations needed
- The repetition implies the message is THAT important
- Makes the viewer feel chosen to receive critical information

**Why it works:** Urgency + selflessness. "This matters so much I'll keep saying it."

## 6. Desirability Hooks

Promise a specific, tangible outcome. These work because people scroll looking for solutions — if you name their exact desire, they stop.

### 6.1 The Ease Promise
**Formula:** "[Achieving desirable outcome] is easier than you think, when you understand this one thing"

- "Losing your belly fat is easier than you think, when you understand this one thing"
- "Reaching $10K/month is easier than you think, when you understand this one thing"
- "Going viral is easier than you think, when you understand this one thing"

**Why it works:** Reduces perceived effort (Hormozi's Value Equation — decrease effort/sacrifice).

### 6.2 The Time Hack
**Formula:** "All it takes is [X] hours per week to completely change your [area] and I'll prove it to you in [X] seconds"

- "All it takes is 4 hours per week to completely change your body and I'll prove it to you in 90 seconds"
- "All it takes is 2 hours per week to completely change your finances and I'll prove it to you in 60 seconds"
- "All it takes is 1 hour per week to completely change your content game and I'll prove it to you in 45 seconds"

**Why it works:** Specific time commitment + proof promise. Reduces both time delay and effort.

### 6.3 The Identity Aspiration
**Formula:** "If you want to become [desirable identity], someone who [is desired for], you have to give up these 3 [things]"

- "If you want to become a magnetic personality, someone who is sought after for what they bring to the world, you have to give up these 3 fears"
- "If you want to become a respected leader, someone people actually follow, you have to give up these 3 beliefs"
- "If you want to become a world-class closer, someone business owners fight to hire, you have to give up these 3 habits"

**Why it works:** Identity-level change > behavior-level change. People want to BECOME someone.

### 6.4 The Secret Shortcut
**Formula:** "Here's an [industry] hack that most people don't know about"

- "Here's a trading hack that most people don't know about"
- "Here's a content creation hack that most people don't know about"
- "Here's a hiring hack that most people don't know about"

**Why it works:** Exclusivity + shortcut promise. Humans are wired to seek the path of least resistance.

### 6.5 The Dialed-In Framework
**Formula:** "You only have to be dialed in on [X] things to be [high performer]"

- "You only have to be dialed in on 3 things to be an elite athlete"
- "You only have to be dialed in on 3 things to be a world-class closer"
- "You only have to be dialed in on 5 things to be a successful content creator"

**Why it works:** Simplification is seductive. "You mean I only need to focus on 3 things?"

### 6.6 The Results Machine
**Formula:** "These [things] [get results] time and time again. Use them in your next [action]"

- "These background audio tracks go viral time and time again. Use them in your next post"
- "These headlines create sales time and time again. Use them in your next ad"
- "These questions close deals time and time again. Use them in your next sales call"

**Why it works:** Proven, repeatable results. Removes the guesswork.

### 6.7 The Quick Win
**Formula:** "Give me [X] seconds and I will [solve your problem]"

- "Give me 30 seconds and I will delete your self-doubt"
- "Give me 60 seconds and I will fix your content strategy"
- "Give me 45 seconds and I will show you why you're not getting clients"

**Why it works:** Ultra-low commitment + bold promise. Hard not to give someone 30 seconds.

### 6.8 The Bold Demand
**Formula:** "For the love of god, whatever you do when [doing action], do not forget to [use technique]"

- "For the love of god, whatever you do during the interview process, do not forget to ask 'the higher performer question'"
- "For the love of god, whatever you do when recording your videos, do not forget to speak in absolutes"
- "For the love of god, whatever you do when responding to a price objection, do not forget to use labeling"

**Why it works:** Extreme urgency + a named technique they haven't heard of. Double curiosity trigger.

## How to Choose the Right Hook

| Your Content Goal | Best Hook Categories |
|---|---|
| Challenge a belief | Contrarian (1.1-1.8) |
| Establish authority | Credibility (2.1-2.7) |
| Create "must watch" urgency | Curiosity (3.1-3.5) |
| Agitate a pain point | Pain/Negative (4.1-4.6) |
| Build connection | Story (5.1-5.5) |
| Promise transformation | Desirability (6.1-6.8) |

**Pro tip:** The strongest hooks combine TWO categories. Example: Credibility + Curiosity = "After 5 years of testing, I found the ONE thing that actually works for [goal]."
