# Your Content Style Profile

Created: 2026-02-15
Based on: Alex Hormozi, Ali Abdaal, Greg Isenberg

## Voice Summary
Confident and direct with a data-driven edge. Breaks down complex ideas into surprisingly simple frameworks. Uses real numbers obsessively — every claim comes with a specific stat. Mixes intensity with casual humor. Treats the viewer as smart but gives them structure they haven't seen before.

## Hook Approach
Lead with a specific number or a contrarian claim. Never ease in. The first sentence should make someone stop scrolling either because the number is surprising or the statement challenges what they believe.

Examples (for a fitness coach targeting tech workers):
- "I work out 3 hours a week and I'm in better shape than people who train 10. Here's why less is more."
- "Stop doing cardio. I know that sounds insane. But here's what the research actually says about fat loss for desk workers."
- "340 tech professionals. 87% still training after 6 months. Here's the one thing they all have in common."
- "Your gym routine is optimized for a fitness influencer, not a software engineer. Let me fix that."
- "I was pre-diabetic at 28 working at Stripe. 18 months later I run a $25k/month fitness company. This is what changed."

## Content Structure
Start with the result or the contrarian claim. Then give context (why this matters, why most people get it wrong). Then deliver the framework or method in clear steps. Close by looping back to the opening claim — prove you delivered on the promise.

Pattern: **Result → Context → Framework → Proof → Loop back**

This is NOT a listicle structure. It's a building argument with a reveal. Each section adds to the previous one.

## Energy & Pacing
- Medium-high energy. Confident, not manic. Think "smart friend explaining something they're excited about" not "motivational speaker"
- Speed up during the framework/method section (momentum)
- Slow down for key insights and proof points (weight)
- Short pauses after bold claims (let them land)
- Conversational speed overall — talking TO someone, not performing for a camera

## Language & Vocabulary
**Use:** "Here's the data," "Let me break this down," "The ROI on this is," "Think of it like," data-driven analogies, tech metaphors (technical debt, optimization, iteration), specific numbers always
**Avoid:** "Beast mode," "grind," "hustle," "no pain no gain," generic motivation, fluffy adjectives (amazing, incredible, revolutionary), "in today's world"
**Profanity level:** Occasional — for emphasis only. A well-placed "that's bullshit" hits harder than constant swearing.
**Humor level:** Dry and self-deprecating. Laugh at yourself, not your audience. Tech-nerdy references are a plus.

## Interest Peaks
Re-engage with data or a pattern interrupt. Never with hype.

Examples:
- "And here's where it gets interesting — the data shows the opposite of what you'd expect."
- "But so many engineers fall into this trap, and it's not their fault."
- "And this is coming from someone who spent 6 years doing it wrong."
- "Now, I tracked this across 340 clients. The pattern is unmistakable."
- "The next part is the one most fitness coaches won't tell you because it's bad for their business model."

## Closing Style
Loop back to the opening claim and prove it was delivered. Don't add new information in the close. End with either:
- A mic-drop one-liner that summarizes the core insight
- A specific actionable next step
- A question that makes the viewer reflect

Never end with "follow for more" or "like and share." If the content was good, they'll follow on their own.

## Style Influences
- From **Alex Hormozi**: The obsession with specific numbers, the confident delivery, the "I'll just tell you exactly what to do" energy. His framework delivery where each step feels inevitable.
- From **Ali Abdaal**: The "evidence-based" approach, the nerdy-casual vibe, making complex things feel accessible and fun. His editing pacing and ability to make educational content entertaining.
- From **Greg Isenberg**: The trend-spotting angle, the "here's what I'm seeing" energy, the business-builder perspective. His ability to make strategic thinking feel conversational.
- **Your own**: The tech insider perspective that none of the above have. The "I was literally that person" relatability. The engineering mindset applied to fitness ("optimize, iterate, measure"). The vulnerability of sharing a health scare story.
