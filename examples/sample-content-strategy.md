## Who I Serve
**Target audience:** Tech professionals (engineers, PMs, founders) aged 25-40 who work 50+ hour weeks and have tried getting fit 3+ times but keep falling off after a few weeks.

**Their #1 problem:** They know WHAT to do (lift weights, eat protein, sleep more) but can't stay consistent because their work schedule is unpredictable and they're mentally drained by end of day.

**Failed attempts:** They've tried: gym memberships they stopped using after month 2, meal prep Sundays that lasted 3 weeks, 5am workout routines that destroyed their sleep, fitness apps that felt generic and unsustainable.

**What they actually want:** A body they're proud of that doesn't require restructuring their entire life. Specifically: look good in a t-shirt, have energy after work, and maintain it for years — not just 12 weeks.

## My Core Beliefs

1. **"Train less, move more" beats "train harder"** — Most tech workers don't need a 5-day gym split. They need 3 focused sessions plus daily walking. I've seen better results with 3x45min than 6x90min for people with demanding jobs.

2. **Fitness influencers are optimizing for the wrong person** — 99% of fitness content is made by people whose JOB is to be fit. Their advice doesn't translate to someone coding 10 hours a day with back-to-back meetings.

3. **Consistency beats intensity, but environment beats willpower** — The answer isn't "just be more disciplined." It's designing your environment so the healthy choice is the easy choice. Meal prep > motivation. Home gym > gym membership for most tech workers.
